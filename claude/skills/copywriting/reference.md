# /copywriting: reference (deep material)

Supporting reference for `SKILL.md`. The skill is operable from SKILL.md alone; this file holds the long-form material kept out of the main file for scannability: the per-asset deep templates (§A), the full canon dives (§B), the complete anti-slop word lists (§C), and the headline-formula library (§D). Same style rule applies here: no em-dash or en-dash anywhere.

---

## §A. Per-asset deep templates

Headline and Hero and Landing are the deepest, because they carry the most weight. Every template still ends at the §7 Audit Gate.

### A1. Headline (the highest-leverage line)
- **Length:** 3 to 12 words. If it needs more, it is two ideas (N4 violation), split or cut.
- **Process:** generate **at least 10** (Caples wrote dozens), each from a different toolbox angle (§5), then audit all and cut to the best 3.
- **Strategy by awareness stage (Schwartz):**
  - *Unaware* then lead with a story or a fact, no product. (You are entering a conversation that is not happening yet.)
  - *Problem-aware* then name the pain in their words (VoC). PAS.
  - *Solution-aware* then name your mechanism, the thing only you do.
  - *Product-aware* then the offer + proof + the believable claim.
  - *Most-aware* then the deal / CTA, get out of the way.
- **Formulas to try** (then make each pass the 3 rules, never ship the formula raw): see §D.
- **The dominant gate:** Rule 3 (nobody-else) + the 2-second test. A headline a competitor could sign is dead on arrival.

### A2. Hero (headline + one support line + CTA)
- **Shape:** one headline (A1) + exactly one supporting line that earns the click + one CTA (A9).
- **The support line's only job** is the slippery slide (C10): get the headline's promise believed, or get the CTA pressed. Not a second idea.
- **Dominant gate:** Rule 1 (the reader must SEE the outcome) + 2-second.
- **Reference DNA:** iPod "1,000 songs in your pocket" (the headline IS the spec made visual). The hero is where metonymy (C5) earns the most.

### A3. Landing section (a multi-section page)
- **One idea per section.** "and" across a section is a leak (C12 / N4). If a section argues two things, it is two sections.
- **Framework by section role:** problem section then PAS; feature section then FAB laddered to meaning; proof section then facts only (C3, Hopkins); close then 4Ps push.
- **Thread a motif** across sections (one repeatable phrase or word), this is what you hand `/artifex` as its motif (N6 to N6).
- **Each section's headline** is its own A1 headline, audited.

### A4. Ad (social / display / paid)
- **Shape:** hook (one falsifiable line) + one conflict (C8, name the enemy) + one CTA.
- **PAS is the default** because cold traffic needs the problem named before it cares.
- **Dominant gate:** Rule 2 (falsify), an ad that makes an unprovable claim gets scrolled past or reported.

### A5. Email
- **Subject:** a falsifiable curiosity gap (Loewenstein, used honestly). Under ~7 words. Must be TRUE (Rule 2), no bait-and-switch.
- **First line:** short and compelling (Sugarman), its only job is to get the second line read. Never open with "I hope this email finds you well."
- **Body:** one idea, slippery slide (C10) top to bottom, one CTA.
- **Frameworks:** PAS (cold) or PASTOR (warm, story-led).

### A6. Push notification
- **Under ~10 words.** One falsifiable fact + one action.
- **No clickbait.** Rule 2 is the gate, the notification must deliver exactly what it implies, or it trains the user to mute you.
- **Specificity wins:** "Your kasir closed Rp 2.4jt today" beats "You have an update."

### A7. Tweet / X post
- **One idea.** Fact-led (C3). The first line is the slippery slide (C10), it must earn the "show more."
- **Open loops are allowed** when honest (the payoff must exist and match).

### A8. Billboard / OOH
- **Under ~7 words.** One visual idea.
- **The 2-second test IS the gate**, you read it at 60mph. If it is not instant, it does not exist.
- **Pair the line with one visual** (C5 metonymy or C11 one-element). Volvo's "six numbers on the speedometer" works because the visual carries half the line.

### A9. Button / CTA microcopy
- **2 to 5 words.** Verb + specific outcome.
- **Banned:** "Submit," "Learn more," "Click here," "Get started" (when it could be specific). Rule 1: the reader should SEE what happens next.
- **Better:** "Start my free trial," "See the live demo," "Hitung hemat saya" (calculate my saving).

## §B. The canon, full dives (the exact idea to borrow from each)

The frameworks are interchangeable scaffolds chosen by audience-awareness; the 3 rules are the sentence-level gate inside whichever scaffold you pick. Borrow the named idea, not the whole book.

- **David Ogilvy** (*Confessions of an Advertising Man*, *Ogilvy on Advertising*): the headline carries most of the spend (he framed it as roughly 80 cents of the dollar, since far more people read the headline than the body); the **Big Idea**; research first; long copy sells when it earns the read; "the consumer is not a moron, she is your wife." The Rolls-Royce "at 60mph the loudest noise is the electric clock" headline is the taste exemplar (he chose it from a motor magazine, taste is selection). **Borrow:** headline primacy, research-first, the Big Idea.
- **Claude Hopkins** (*Scientific Advertising*, 1923, Ch.7 "Being Specific"): **specifics over superlatives.** "Platitudes and generalities roll off the human understanding like water from a duck. They leave no impression whatever." A specific claim is either a truth or a lie, so it is believed; actual figures are not discounted ("reduced 25 percent," "used by 52 nations," "1,018 experiments"). Test everything; reason-why copy; salesmanship in print. **Borrow:** the specificity-over-superlatives law, which is Harry's "point, don't talk," a century early.
- **Eugene Schwartz** (*Breakthrough Advertising*, 1966): the deepest theory in the canon. **(a)** copy **channels existing mass desire**, it cannot create it. **(b) 5 Stages of Awareness:** Unaware then Problem-Aware then Solution-Aware then Product-Aware then Most-Aware; the headline strategy changes per stage (§A1). **(c) 5 Stages of Market Sophistication:** as a market hears the same claim repeatedly you escalate Direct claim then Bigger claim then **Mechanism** then Better mechanism then **Identification**. **Borrow:** awareness stage is THE input that selects framework + headline. This is "who are you talking to" made rigorous.
- **Joe Sugarman** (*The Adweek Copywriting Handbook*): the **slippery slide**, every element exists to get the next element read; the first sentence short and compelling; "sell on emotion, justify with logic"; seeds of curiosity. **Borrow:** the slide as a flow checkpoint between every two sentences.
- **Gary Halbert** (*The Boron Letters*): the **starving crowd**, given any advantage to sell a burger, take the starving crowd (market demand beats product and copy). A-pile vs B-pile mail. **Borrow:** market/demand selection precedes copy, "who is hungry for this?" is an input, not an afterthought.
- **John Caples** (*Tested Advertising Methods*): headlines win on self-interest, news, or curiosity. "They Laughed When I Sat Down at the Piano... But When I Started to Play!" (US School of Music, written c.1925 at Ruthrauff & Ryan, ran 1926). Write **dozens** of headlines, then test. **Borrow:** generate-many-then-select (mirrors the rewrite loop).
- **David Abbott** (Abbott Mead Vickers): Britain's finest long-copy writer. Storytelling + integrity; "words are servants of the argument"; **read it aloud**; research first; a cliche trash-bin. **Borrow:** the read-aloud test (§7) and the cliche bin (§C).
- **Luke Sullivan** (*Hey Whipple, Squeeze This*): "draw a line down the middle, write two conflicts." The One big simple idea. **Borrow:** the conflict-generation drill (C8).
- **Robert Collier** (*The Robert Collier Letter Book*): "enter the conversation already going on in the prospect's mind." **Borrow:** the literal definition of audience-entry (Phase 1).
- **Bill Bernbach / DDB** (VW "Think Small," "Lemon"): honesty + the product truth + wit; less is more. **Borrow:** the one-element / radical-simplicity instinct (C11), the "I never read The Economist" lineage.
- **Joanna Wiebe / Copyhackers (modern VoC):** the best copy is **found, not written**. Mine reviews, support tickets, sales calls for the customer's exact words; research is roughly two-thirds of the work; the "so what / says who" tests. **Borrow:** the VoC input step (Phase 1), paste reviews/competitor copy then extract real phrases. The most important modern practice Harry implies but does not systematize.
- **Alex Hormozi** (*$100M Offers*): the **Value Equation** = (Dream Outcome x Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) / (Time Delay x Effort & Sacrifice). Make the numerator big, the denominator small; the bottom of the equation is where most offers are won. **Borrow:** a value-articulation pass to pressure-test the offer before writing (Phase 2). Harry's "believable claim" is the Perceived-Likelihood term.
- **Curiosity gap** (George Loewenstein, "The Psychology of Curiosity," 1994): a perceived gap between what we know and want to know creates a need to fill. **Borrow:** the open loop, used HONESTLY, guarded by Rule 2 + sincerity, never clickbait.

### B.synthesis: where Harry sits
Harry Dry is Hopkins + Ogilvy (specificity, research, the big idea, taste) re-expressed for the landing-page / Twitter era, with the sentence-level rigor the old masters left implicit turned into a memorable 3-question test, plus Sugarman's flow and Bernbach's simplicity. What the canon adds that the video under-specifies: **Schwartz's awareness/sophistication stages** (how to choose the message) and **Wiebe's VoC** (where the raw material comes from). The skill's edge = the video's testable simplicity x the canon's strategic depth.

## §C. The complete anti-slop word lists

Any hit is an instant audit fail (SKILL §8). Scan EN and ID before scoring anything.

### C1. EN banned (unfalsifiable adjectives / empty intensifiers)
seamless · world-class · cutting-edge · state-of-the-art · robust · powerful · elevate · unlock · revolutionize · revolutionary · game-changing · game-changer · next-level · best-in-class · industry-leading · supercharge · effortless · delightful (as a claim) · innovative · innovation · leverage (as a verb) · synergy · holistic · turnkey · solutions (as a noun for "stuff we sell") · empower · streamline · optimize (vague) · transform (vague) · disrupt · bespoke (when generic) · curated (when generic) · premium (unearned) · "the future of X" · meticulously · thrilled · passionate · "we are committed to" · "second to none" · "one-stop shop."

### C2. EN banned openers + cadences
"In today's fast-paced world..." · "In an increasingly digital age..." · "Imagine a world where..." · "We are excited / thrilled to announce..." · "It is not just X, it is Y" (unless genuinely earned by a real contrast) · "Whether you are X or Y..." · "Say goodbye to X and hello to Y" · "Look no further" · the **rule-of-three vibe list** ("fast, simple, and secure") · **em-dash and en-dash** (use comma / colon / parentheses / line break).

### C3. ID banned (empty superlatives + slop)
solusi terbaik · solusi tepat · terpercaya · paling terpercaya · berkualitas tinggi · kualitas terbaik · harga terjangkau · harga bersaing · pelayanan memuaskan · pelayanan prima · nomor satu · nomor 1 · terdepan · terlengkap · terbaik di kelasnya · profesional dan amanah · sudah berpengalaman bertahun-tahun · melayani dengan sepenuh hati · kepuasan Anda prioritas kami · "tunggu apa lagi?" · "buruan sebelum kehabisan!" (with no real reason) · "dijamin puas."

### C4. ID banned openers
"Di era digital seperti sekarang ini..." · "Seperti yang kita ketahui bersama..." · "Tidak bisa dipungkiri..." · "Di zaman yang serba cepat ini..." · "Sebagai salah satu yang terbaik...".

### C5. The positive test (must PASS)
see-it (Rule 1) · true (Rule 2) · ours-only (Rule 3) · one idea (N4) · a real fact under it (C3 toolbox) · sincere (N5). If a line clears the bans AND passes this, it is a candidate for the §7 table.

## §D. The headline-formula library

Formulas are starting points, NEVER ship one raw. Every formula output must still pass the 3 rules (§2). Generate many, cut to the best (Caples).

| Formula | Shape | Example direction |
|---|---|---|
| **The 4 U's check** | Useful + Urgent + Unique + Ultra-specific | score a draft against all four |
| **How-to-without** | "How to [outcome] without [pain]" | "How to close the books without a single spreadsheet" |
| **Do-X-like-Y** | "[Do desirable thing] like [aspirational group]" | "Run your warung like a franchise" |
| **The believable number** | a small, specific, provable gain | "Turn 1% into 2%" not "10x your revenue" |
| **Point-don't-talk** (C2) | replace the adjective with a pointable fact | "Reads on the tube" not "well-read" |
| **Metonymy** (C5) | the spec as a visual object | "1,000 songs in your pocket" |
| **Conflict / enemy** (C8) | name the status quo it kills | "Meetings that should have been a video" |
| **Reframe the cost** (C9) | cost becomes saving; expand the timeframe | "22,000 hours writing. Spend two learning how." |
| **One-element** (C11) | strip to the single load-bearing element | "We make jeans." |
| **The owned phrase** | the line only this brand can sign (Rule 3) | "Designed to be deleted" (Hinge) |

> Rule of One over all of it: one reader, one idea, one promise, one CTA. A headline that needs a comma-spliced second idea is two headlines.

## §E. The rewrite loop (the operating discipline, expanded)

You cannot write simply, you can only rewrite simply. The worked centerpiece (the "Throw money and pray / Learn copywriting" ad) took roughly 25 rewrites over 2 to 3 days:

1. **Seed** from a real fact or a years-old note ("the difference between 1% and 2% is not 1%, it is 100%").
2. **Two conflicts** (Sullivan): draw a line down the middle, write the corporate way vs the copywriting way.
3. **Borrow a layout** that already works (the VW "prepare your car for winter" structure).
4. **Spitball headers**, many of them, deliberately allowing bad ones (the freedom to do it wrong unlocks the good one).
5. **Tighten** the long line ("by hook or by crook" out), cut every non-working word (Kaplan).
6. **Land** on the line ("Throw money and pray / Learn copywriting").
7. **Feedback** from people whose taste you respect; show 3 variants, take the one sentence they point to, graft it in.
8. **Design last**, in the final medium (write the ad in Figma, the newsletter in the newsletter). Copy and design are one (N10).
9. **The done test:** walk around the garden, come back, "I cannot take anything away from this. One line flows to the next." Ship at a 9-out-of-10 bar, not before. Your standards are your work.
