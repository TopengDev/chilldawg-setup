# Channel playbooks (per-channel launch-day tactics + waitlist mechanics)

Reference for the SKILL.md §3 ORB inventory. Each channel below carries a launch-day DO and a launch-day DONT, plus which ORB class it is and how it funnels into owned. Everything a rented / borrowed channel does should drive attention INTO an owned surface (the audience you keep). Kept out of SKILL.md so the load-bearing rules stay load-fast.

Every drafted post here is a DRAFT Christopher fires (SKILL.md §0.5), carries zero vote-ask language (§0.4), and clears the anti-slop floor (§10). X mechanics defer to `references/x-launch-runbook.md`.

## Owned channels (the audience you keep)

### Email list (owned, usually the primary T-0 announce)
- **Role:** the highest-intent launch-day channel. These people opted in; they convert best.
- **DO:** send a short, specific announcement on T-0 with ONE clear CTA to the product. Name the ONE new thing + who it is for (the §10 specificity test). If PH is in scope, invite them to "come see it + tell us what you think" (never "go upvote").
- **DONT:** bury the CTA under three paragraphs, send a generic "big news!" with no substance, or ask for upvotes (§0.4). Do not send the launch email to a cold purchased list (spam-flag + it does not convert).
- **Funnel:** it IS owned; the job is to convert readers into product users + keep them subscribed.

### Blog (owned)
- **Role:** the durable launch artifact (the post that ranks + gets linked to later).
- **DO:** publish a real launch post on T-0 (what it is, why you built it, who it is for, how to try it). This is the canonical link the other channels point at.
- **DONT:** make it a press release. Write it as the honest story, not "we are thrilled to announce".
- **Funnel:** it is owned; embed the signup / product CTA and the waitlist capture.

### Community (Slack / Discord / WhatsApp group) (owned, or borrowed if it is someone else's)
- **Role:** your warmest, most-engaged people; strong for early launch-day velocity.
- **DO:** give your OWN community a heads-up + first look; they are the people who will genuinely engage. For an Indonesia launch, a WhatsApp group + local community is often a stronger lever than a US board (SKILL.md Indonesian-market note).
- **DONT:** spam someone ELSE'S community with a launch drop; if it is a borrowed community, add value + follow its rules, do not treat it as a billboard.
- **Funnel:** drive them to the product + capture emails.

## Rented channels (visibility you borrow from a platform)

### X / Twitter (rented)
- **Role:** the launch thread + real-time amplification, especially for a dev / crypto audience.
- **DO:** post a launch thread (hero image on tweet 1, text reply-chain for the rest). Full mechanics + the 280-char cap + the automation constraints are in `references/x-launch-runbook.md`. Draft it, Christopher fires it.
- **DONT:** use the native multi-tweet composer or assume reply-image attach works (both broken, see the runbook). Do not auto-post or ask for RTs/votes.
- **Funnel:** every thread links to the owned surface (waitlist / product / blog).

### LinkedIn (rented)
- **Role:** reach for a B2B / professional audience + recruiters.
- **DO:** a single high-value post, story or insight first (why you built this, the problem it solves), with ONE link. Max 2 to 3 hashtags.
- **DONT:** a hashtag wall, a "thrilled to announce" opener, or engagement-bait ("Agree? Thoughts?"). No emoji-opener slop.
- **Funnel:** the one link goes to the owned surface.

### Reddit (rented / borrowed, high-risk if done wrong)
- **Role:** a relevant subreddit can send real, high-intent traffic IF you respect the culture.
- **DO:** post an honest, self-aware Show-and-tell in a genuinely relevant subreddit, disclose you built it, engage the comments as a human. Read the subreddit rules first (many ban self-promo).
- **DONT:** vote-ring, sockpuppet, or drop-and-run. Reddit punishes inauthentic promotion hard, and a downvoted/removed post + a burned account is worse than not posting.
- **Funnel:** a soft link in the post / comments to the product.

### Hacker News (rented)
- **Role:** a "Show HN" can send a large, technical, high-intent spike if it resonates.
- **DO:** post as **Show HN** with an honest, non-clickbait title (what it is, plainly). Be present in the comments, answer technical questions directly, take criticism gracefully.
- **DONT:** vote-ring or ask friends to upvote (HN detects vote-rings and penalizes / bans; same spirit as PH §0.4). No marketing-speak title; HN allergic to hype.
- **Funnel:** the link is the product / a real writeup; capture emails on-site.

### BetaList / launch-directory sites (rented)
- **Role:** early-adopter exposure + a waitlist boost pre-launch.
- **DO:** use them to seed the waitlist BEFORE the main launch moment; treat them as top-of-funnel for owned.
- **DONT:** treat a directory listing as the launch; it is a feeder, not the moment.
- **Funnel:** straight into the waitlist.

## Borrowed channels (someone else's audience, the shortcut to getting noticed)

- **Role:** instant credibility + reach you have not built yet: a podcast interview, a newsletter feature, a guest post, a reviewer / influencer who covers you, a launch-day hunter with reach, an affiliate / referral loop.
- **DO (be proactive, not passive):** list the industry voices your audience already follows; pitch a genuine win-win (via `/outreach`, which drafts the person-specific ask); use audience-overlap tools (e.g. SparkToro, Listen Notes) to find the right ones; set up an affiliate / referral incentive for ongoing reach. Send a genuinely useful product to a relevant reviewer and hope they like it (a real free-product-to-reviewer play can convert borrowed attention into a real audience; treat the outcome as a possibility, never a guaranteed result).
- **DONT:** buy fake reach, pay for a vote-ring, or expect a one-off borrowed spike to stick. Borrowed attention only works if you convert it into an owned relationship (capture the email, get them on the list).
- **Funnel:** every borrowed touch must point at an owned capture; otherwise you paid (in time or product) for attention that leaks away.

## Waitlist / early-access mechanics (owned, the pre-launch audience builder)

A waitlist is how you build a warm owned audience BEFORE the launch moment so the moment has somewhere to land (the #1 flop cause is launching into a vacuum, SKILL.md §8). The levers below are **patterns, not guaranteed outcomes**: they raise the odds, they do not promise a number. Never attach an unsourced "this drove $X" claim (SKILL.md rule 7).

- **Exclusivity / invite-only:** a gated "request access" frame can create scarcity and a reason to share. Lever, not magic.
- **Referral position-jumping:** let a signup move up the queue by referring others (the classic viral-loop mechanic). Raises sharing incentive; the effect varies wildly by product.
- **Batched invites (5 to 10% at a time):** release access in waves rather than all at once. This throttles load, creates repeated "you are in" moments, and lets you fix issues between batches (SKILL.md §4 Phase 4).
- **A concrete "why now":** a real reason the waitlist exists (limited early spots, a genuine capacity limit, an early-adopter perk) beats a generic "join the waitlist".
- **Warm it, do not just collect it:** an unengaged waitlist is a cold list by launch day (and its votes get newbie-filtered on PH, §6). Send the list occasional real value between signup and launch so they are warm and PH-active when T-0 hits.

Recipe (repeatable):
1. Stand up the waitlist landing page early (SKILL.md §5, T-30). Capture email; store + verify a test signup.
2. Give it a real "why now" + one honest exclusivity or referral lever.
3. Feed it from the rented / borrowed channels + directory sites (BetaList) as top-of-funnel.
4. Warm it with periodic value so it is engaged, not dormant, by launch day.
5. On T-0, the warm waitlist is your primary owned announce (email, above) and your primed-supporter pool (PH §6c).

Illustrative note: the invite-only + 1:1 onboarding pattern (a well-known scarcity play) is a real lever some products used to build demand; treat it as a pattern to consider, not a promised result, and never cite a specific unverifiable outcome figure for it.
